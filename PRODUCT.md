# Product

## Register

brand

> Default for design work is `brand` — the public customer site (home, portfolio,
> service areas, estimate) is the surface that wins jobs and lives or dies on craft.
> The internal `/admin` staff app is genuinely a second surface; treat it as
> `product` register when working there (employee list, scheduling, timesheets).

## Users

Colorado Springs homeowners (and the Colorado springs tree service business owner that the tree is too much for them) with a tree
that's too big, too dead, storm-damaged, or too close to the house to deal with
themselves. They usually arrive stressed — a hazard limb over the roof, a tree
down after a storm — on a phone, comparing a couple of local options, wanting a
fast honest answer. The job to be done: get the tree removed or trimmed safely,
without overpaying and without a flaky rotating crew.

Secondary users: the owner-operator (and future hires) using `/admin` to manage
accounts, scheduling, and timesheets as the LLC grows.

## Product Purpose

Turn local searches and referrals into estimate requests and phone calls for a
solo professional tree climber. The site sells one thing: a trained climber who
quotes the job and climbs it himself, roped and rigged by hand. Success is
qualified estimate submissions and calls — not traffic, not time-on-page. The
internal staff app exists so the business side (people, shifts, hours) stays off
spreadsheets as it scales.

## Brand Personality

Rugged, precise, trustworthy. A real climber with ropes and gear, not a sales
office. Voice is straight-talking and local — no fluff, no upsell, plain about
what a tree needs and what it costs. The craft is premium (technical rope and
rigging work, careful with your property) but the presentation is earned, never
flashy. Emotional goal: confidence and trust — "this guy clearly knows what he's
doing, he won't wreck my yard or my wallet."

## Anti-references

- **Cheap local-SEO template.** Badge soup, cluttered hero, keyword-stuffed copy,
  stock "tree service" imagery, five fake review widgets. The opposite of trust.
- **Corporate franchise (TruGreen / Davey).** Impersonal green-corporate chain
  look, stock photos, call-center vibe. This is one person, and it should feel
  like one person.
- **Flashy / over-designed.** Heavy scroll animation, loud gradients, anything
  that's slow or hard to read on a phone in bright Colorado sun. Restraint reads
  as competence here.

## Design Principles

- **One climber, start to finish.** The person who quotes is the person who
  climbs. The design should feel owner-operated and personal — first-person voice,
  real face, no fake "our team."
- **Show the rope work.** Proof beats adjectives. Real photos of real jobs carry
  the credibility; the portfolio is the engine, not a footnote.
- **Make the call one tap away.** Phone, text, and the estimate form are the whole
  conversion. Keep them obvious, sun-readable, and reachable from any screen
  without hunting.
- **Earned premium, not flashy premium.** Signal skill through clarity, sharp
  typography, and restraint — never through animation or gloss.
- **Fast or it's broken.** Outdoor mobile users on cell data. Instant load is a
  feature; two seconds is a lost job.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Mobile-first with sun-readable contrast (lean toward the high end of
AA for body text), large tap targets for thumb use, and full keyboard/focus
support on the estimate form. Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion` (the public
pages render fully without JS; any motion is CSS-only with a reduced-motion
fallback). Phone and email must always be plain, selectable, tappable links —
never image-only.
